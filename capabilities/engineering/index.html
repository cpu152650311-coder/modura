Design Philosophy

Engineering-First Approach

In modular construction, engineering decisions made at the concept stage determine everything that follows. Unlike traditional construction where design and engineering run on parallel tracks, our engineering-first approach front-loads structural analysis, MEP coordination, and fire strategy before a single steel section is cut.

This means every module is analysed for five load cases — factory lift, transport, crane, stacking, and in-service — before production begins. Clash detection in the BIM model eliminates fit issues that would otherwise surface on site. Fire compartmentation is engineered into the module geometry, not retrofitted after assembly.

  • All six engineering disciplines engaged from Day 1 of design
  • BIM federated model updated in real time across all disciplines
  • Five load cases analysed per module before fabrication release
  • Full MEP coordination completed in the digital model, not on site
  • Fire strategy integrated into structural and architectural design

Digital Delivery

BIM & Digital Engineering

Our ISO 19650-compliant BIM processes ensure every stakeholder works from a single source of truth, from concept design through to digital twin handover.

ISO 19650 Compliance

Full BIM Level 2 delivery in accordance with ISO 19650-1 and 19650-2. Common Data Environment (CDE) with controlled information exchange at each project stage.

LOD 500 Modelling

Every module modelled to LOD 500 as-built fidelity: structural connections, MEP routing, fire stopping, and architectural finishes all represented in the federated model.

4D Sequencing

Construction sequencing linked to the BIM model enables logistics optimisation: module delivery phasing, crane positioning, and just-in-time site assembly all visualised before mobilisation.

Digital Twin Handover

Complete as-built BIM dataset delivered at handover for facilities management. Asset registers, maintenance schedules, and warranty tracking linked to the model.

Building Services

MEP Pre-Integration

Factory-installed mechanical, electrical, and plumbing systems eliminate the coordination chaos of traditional site work and deliver higher quality through controlled assembly conditions.

HVAC Systems

Ducted ventilation with heat recovery (MVHR), fan coil units, or VRF systems pre-installed and balanced in-factory. Only inter-module duct connections required on site.

Fire Suppression

Life Safety & Comfort

Fire Engineering & Acoustic Performance

Two disciplines where modular construction demands exceptional rigour. Our fire engineering and acoustic design teams work together to deliver safety without compromise.

Fire Engineering

BS EN 13501 & 120-Minute Protection

Our fire engineering strategy is built into the module design from the start. Compartmentation boundaries are defined at the module interface, with fire-resistant boards, intumescent seals, and cavity barriers integrated during factory assembly — not retrofitted on site.

Every project undergoes computational fluid dynamics (CFD) smoke modelling and timed evacuation analysis using Pathfinder or equivalent software. Fire resistance ratings are verified through full-scale furnace testing to BS EN 13501-2, with loadbearing modules achieving up to 120 minutes REI classification.

  • Up to 120 min structural fire resistance (REI 120)
  • BS EN 13501-1 reaction-to-fire classification throughout
  • CFD smoke modelling and ASET/RSET evacuation analysis
  • Intumescent cavity barriers at every module joint
  • Approved Document B and BS 9999 compliant strategies
Acoustic & Thermal

DnT,w + Ctr ≥ 45dB, U = 0.15

Modular buildings face unique acoustic challenges at the module-to-module interface. Our double-wall separation system delivers airborne sound insulation exceeding DnT,w + Ctr 45dB between dwelling units — verified by UKAS-accredited laboratory testing to BS EN ISO 16283-1.

Thermal performance is engineered for long-term energy cost reduction. Our standard building envelope achieves U-values of 0.15 W/m²K for walls and 0.13 W/m²K for roofs, with thermal bridging minimised through continuous insulation strategies and thermally broken connections at all module interfaces.

  • DnT,w + Ctr ≥ 45dB airborne between units
  • Impact sound L'nT,w ≤ 52dB on separating floors
  • Wall U-value 0.15 W/m²K, roof 0.13 W/m²K
  • PSI-value thermal bridging analysis per module detail
  • 25% above IECC / Part L baseline energy performance
