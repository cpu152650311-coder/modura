How It Works

Factory Precision, Site Assembly

Panelized systems strike an optimal balance between factory quality control and on-site flexibility. Panels arrive pre-cut, pre-insulated, and pre-sheathed. Site crews erect the structural enclosure in days rather than weeks, then complete MEP first fix, plasterboard, and finishes using conventional techniques — but starting from a dimensionally perfect shell.

  • Steel or timber frame — choose the right material for your project
  • Windows and external doors pre-installed in wall panels at the factory
  • Service zones pre-formed: conduit routes, back boxes, plumbing chases
  • Cladding support rails factory-fixed, reducing working-at-height site time
  • BIM model drives every panel: 10,000+ components digitally coordinated before fabrication

Advantages

Why Choose Panelized

Lower Transport Cost

Flat-packed panels ship up to 3x the building area per truck compared to volumetric modules. For projects with long logistics distances or constrained site access, panelized systems dramatically reduce the transport cost per square metre — often the deciding factor for regional and remote projects.

Design Flexibility

Because MEP and finishes are completed on site, panelized projects accommodate design changes later in the programme than volumetric. Room layouts, service routes, and finish specifications can be adjusted after panels are erected — valuable for tenant-fit-out phases and speculative developments where the end user is not yet known.

Hybrid-Compatible

Panelized systems combine seamlessly with volumetric modules in a hybrid approach. Use volumetric for kitchens, bathrooms, and plant rooms (wet, service-intensive spaces) and panelized for bedrooms, living areas, and corridors (dry, flexible spaces). One BIM model coordinates both systems — single-source design responsibility.

Rapid Enclosure

A trained 4-person crew erects 80–120 m² of external wall per day. A 4-storey, 2,000 m² building can be structurally enclosed and weather-tight in under 3 weeks. This compresses the programme's critical path, unlocks earlier MEP and finishes start dates, and reduces the site's exposure to weather risk during the most vulnerable construction phase.
