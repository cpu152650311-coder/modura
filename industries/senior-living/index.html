Industry Challenges

Senior Living Development Challenges

Senior living facilities must balance hospitality-grade comfort with clinical-grade safety — all while controlling costs in a sector where margins are tight and regulatory scrutiny is intense.

Resident Safety During Construction

Phased developments adjacent to occupied buildings create unacceptable risk profiles. Construction noise, dust, and contractor movement compromise resident wellbeing and infection control protocols.

Accessibility Compliance

Every element — door widths, turning circles, threshold details, grab rail backing, tap forces — must meet BS 8300 and Part M. Site-built variations from specification create compliance risk that is expensive to rectify.

Complex MEP Requirements

Senior living demands sophisticated mechanical and electrical systems: nurse call, wander management, access control, HEPA filtration, emergency power, and assistive technology — all of which create coordination challenges for traditional sequential trades.

Rising Construction Costs

Labour-intensive fit-out — wet rooms, wide corridors, enhanced acoustic separation, specialised MEP — drives per-square-metre costs well above standard residential. Fixed-price factory manufacturing eliminates on-site cost escalation.

Applications

Senior Living Typologies

Our modular system scales across the full continuum of care, from active adult communities to high-acuity nursing environments.

Independent Living Apartments

Self-contained 1 and 2-bedroom apartments for active seniors. Open-plan layouts with adaptable kitchens, level-access showers, and private outdoor terraces. Community hub modules provide lounges, libraries, and fitness spaces.

Assisted Living Suites

Studio and 1-bedroom suites with 24-hour care capability. Each suite includes a fully accessible en-suite wet room, kitchenette, and integrated nurse call. Staff base modules with medications room, sluice, and equipment store serve each cluster of 8–12 suites.

Memory Care Households

Small-scale households of 10–14 residents with domestic-scale kitchen, dining, and living spaces. Continuous loop corridor design eliminates dead ends; colour-coded wayfinding integrated into interior finishes; secure garden modules with raised planters and sensory planting.

CCRC Communities

Continuing Care Retirement Communities combining independent living, assisted living, memory care, and skilled nursing on a single campus. Modular construction enables phased delivery — open independent living first to generate revenue while care wings complete.
